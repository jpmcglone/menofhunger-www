# Men of Hunger Success Playbook

Success for Men of Hunger is not just growth in signups. Real success is a repeatable loop where people post, get meaningful replies, come back daily, and invite others because the community feels valuable and trustworthy.

Below are 10 things you should probably do to maximize your odds of success.

## 1) Make New Users Feel a Live Community in 60 Seconds

**What to do:** Ensure every new user sees an active, relevant feed immediately (no empty-home dead ends).  
**Why it works:** Reddit, X, and Discord win early because new users instantly see activity and social proof.  
**First step for Men of Hunger:** Build a fallback feed path: if follow-feed is sparse, inject high-quality trending posts from 2-3 core topics.

## 2) Force Fast Personalization During Onboarding

**What to do:** Require users to follow people and choose topics before landing on Home.  
**Why it works:** X and Strava improve early retention when users quickly shape their own timeline.  
**First step for Men of Hunger:** Add an onboarding checkpoint: follow at least 5 accounts and pick at least 3 interests/topics.

## 3) Treat Replies + Mentions as the Core Retention Engine

**What to do:** Prioritize reply threads and mention loops as the main reason to return.  
**Why it works:** Conversation loops (X replies, Reddit comments, Discord mentions) create high-frequency re-engagement.  
**First step for Men of Hunger:** Audit notification delivery for replies/mentions and ensure these are highest-priority push events.

## 4) Build Daily Habit Loops Around Check-Ins and Streaks

**What to do:** Make check-ins, streak continuity, and streak recovery a primary product lane.  
**Why it works:** Duolingo-style streak mechanics and faith app daily practices (YouVersion/Hallow) create predictable return behavior.  
**First step for Men of Hunger:** Add a daily “streak at risk” reminder and a lightweight streak-save mechanic for premium users later.

## 5) Keep Free Tier Truly Useful, Make Premium Additive

**What to do:** Preserve strong free value; premium should feel like acceleration, not access ransom.  
**Why it works:** Discord/Reddit/Substack retain trust by avoiding hard paywalls on core community utility.  
**First step for Men of Hunger:** Define and publish a simple premium value table (ad-free, enhanced perks, premium-only content), and verify core posting/replying remains strong for free users.

## 6) Use Gated Content Teasers That Create Curiosity, Not Friction

**What to do:** Show tasteful previews and transparent “what you unlock” messaging for gated posts/replies.  
**Why it works:** Users convert better when they understand value before purchase (not after frustration).  
**First step for Men of Hunger:** Standardize premium/verified gate cards to include: preview snippet, exact unlock benefit, and one clear CTA.

## 7) Concentrate Community Density Before Expanding Categories

**What to do:** Focus activity into a few high-signal topics instead of spreading users thin.  
**Why it works:** Early communities fail when too many rooms/tags are empty; density beats breadth.  
**First step for Men of Hunger:** Pick the top 3 strategic topics and feature them in feed, onboarding, and weekly prompts for the next quarter.

## 8) Establish Strong, Visible Moderation and Culture Rules

**What to do:** Make community standards explicit, enforce consistently, and communicate outcomes clearly.  
**Why it works:** Trust and safety are growth multipliers in faith/mission communities where tone quality matters.  
**First step for Men of Hunger:** Publish a short “How we discuss here” policy in-app and add a transparent moderation escalation flow.

## 9) Add a Dual-Sided Referral Loop

**What to do:** Incentivize invites with rewards for both inviter and invitee.  
**Why it works:** Dropbox- and Notion-style referral mechanics scale acquisition at lower trust cost than paid ads alone.  
**First step for Men of Hunger:** Ship a referral MVP: invite link + successful signup attribution + reward (coins/premium trial) to both users.

## 10) Optimize for Retention Quality, Not Vanity Growth

**What to do:** Run execution by retention and conversation health metrics, not only installs or MAU spikes.  
**Why it works:** Sustainable communities are built on depth (replies, return cadence, creator consistency), not superficial top-line growth.  
**First step for Men of Hunger:** Create a weekly scorecard with: D1/D7 retention, replies per post, % posters who receive a reply, mention notification open rate, and 4-week creator retention.

---

## 90-Day Focus (Top 3 Priorities)

1. **Onboarding + Empty Feed Fixes**  
   Ship mandatory follow/topic onboarding and fallback feed injection to remove dead-start experiences.

2. **Conversation Return Loops**  
   Tune reply/mention notifications, improve thread visibility, and target “every meaningful post gets a response.”

3. **Habit + Trust Foundation**  
   Strengthen check-in/streak loop and publish clear community norms/moderation behavior to protect culture quality as growth increases.
